## The Problem: Why Most Developer Resumes Get Rejected

Most IT professionals and developers lose opportunities at the **very first stage** — before a human even reads their resume.

### Why Resumes Get Filtered Out Automatically

Modern companies in the **USA, UK, Canada, and Australia** use **Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS)** to filter hundreds of applications before a recruiter reviews them.

Your resume gets **silently rejected** if:

- Keywords don't match the job description
- Formatting breaks ATS parsing (tables, columns, graphics)
- File type or structure isn't ATS-compatible
- Skills section is missing or poorly structured
- Job titles don't align with industry-standard terminology

**ATS filters reject up to 75% of resumes automatically** — even from highly qualified candidates.

---

## What Is ATS Optimization and Why It Matters

**ATS (Applicant Tracking System)** is software used by companies to manage recruiting. Systems like **Workday, Greenhouse, Lever, Taleo, iCIMS, and BambooHR** are widely used across:

- Fortune 500 companies in the **USA**
- Tech firms in **UK and Europe**
- Product companies in **Canada and Australia**

An ATS-optimized resume:

- Uses the **right keywords** from job descriptions
- Has **clean, parseable formatting** (no columns, tables, or graphics)
- Includes **standard section headings** (Experience, Education, Skills)
- Has **quantifiable achievements**, not just job duties
- Is saved in the **correct file format** (usually .docx or .pdf)
